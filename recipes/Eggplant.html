Stuffed Eggplant

Description

Stuffed Eggplant, also known as "Imam Bayildi" in some Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines, is a hearty and flavorful dish where eggplants are hollowed out and filled with a savory mixture. The eggplants are typically cut in half lengthwise, and the flesh is scooped out to create a "boat" that can hold the filling.

The filling varies by region but often includes a mixture of saut√©ed vegetables like onions, tomatoes, bell peppers, and garlic, along with ground meat such as beef, lamb, or turkey. Herbs and spices like cumin, paprika, oregano, and parsley are commonly used to enhance the flavor. Some variations include rice or bulgur, nuts like pine nuts or walnuts, and even raisins for a sweet contrast.

In vegetarian versions, the filling might be made entirely of vegetables, herbs, and grains, often accompanied by a generous amount of olive oil and sometimes topped with cheese like feta or Parmesan.

After being stuffed, the eggplants are usually baked until tender, allowing the flavors to meld together. The final dish is rich, aromatic, and full of contrasting textures, with the soft, tender eggplant complementing the robust and flavorful filling. It's often served as a main course or a substantial side dish, garnished with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lemon juice for added brightness.

ingredients

  • 1 (1 1/2 pound) eggplant, halved lengthwise
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 pound sweet Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • 1 cup tomato-basil pasta sauce
  • 3/4 cup Italian seasoned panko bread crumbs, divided
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided

Direction

  1. Step 1

    Gather ingredients. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil.

  2. Step 2

    Cut around the edge of the flesh in each eggplant half using a sharp paring knife, leaving a 1/2-inch border. Scoop out flesh using a spoon, leaving 1/2-inch-thick shell; reserve flesh and chop into bite-size pieces.

  3. Step 3

    Brush shells with 2 tablespoons olive oil and season with salt and pepper; set aside.

  4. Step 4

    Heat remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large, deep skillet over medium high heat. Add sausage, onion, mushrooms, and garlic; cook and stir until sausage is evenly brown. Stir in reserved chopped eggplant and basil. Add pasta sauce; cook and stir for 5 minutes. Mix in 1/2 cup of panko and 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese.

  5. Step 5

    Stuff mixture into eggplant shells. Combine remaining panko and cheese in a small bowl; sprinkle over stuffing.

  6. Step 6

    Bake in preheated oven until eggplant is tender, 45 to 50 minutes.
